The Problem: Northern Colorado's Food System

Despite rapid population growth, Northern Colorado's local food economy remains stagnant — with declining direct-to-consumer sales and only 1% of agricultural receipts staying local, far below comparable counties nationwide.

🎯 Section 6

2023 Community Action Priorities

Four strategic goals with specific actions and tactics — synthesized from community surveys, producer assessments, working groups, and focus groups. This action plan is the roadmap for the next phase of implementation.

🤲
Food Sovereignty & Access
Equitable food system prioritizing community health and equal rights for all
🗣️
Community Outreach & Education
Inclusive strategies to educate and engage the community
🌱
Production Growth & Land Access
Support production growth, land tenure, and market access
Goal #1
✊
Elevate & Empower the Voices of Marginalized People & Groups
  • Implement a Food Equity & Policy Council for Northern Colorado
  • Facilitate buy-in from local municipalities & stakeholders
  • Long-term organizational support to sustain engagement (5+ years)
🤲 Food Sovereignty & Access
Goal #2
🥬
Increase Access to Local Foods Among All Community Members
  • Increase SNAP/WIC/Double Up Food Buck outlets
  • Expand cooperation with big box stores & local retailers
  • Develop novel market strategies (mobile markets, food hubs)
🤲 🗣️ Food Sovereignty + Outreach
Goal #3
📚
Increase Consumer Education & Awareness of the Local Food Economy
  • Food curriculum development in local schools & afterschool programs
  • Tell the stories of local producers in engaging, humanizing formats
  • Create program awareness campaigns & local resource guides
🗣️ Community Outreach & Education
Goal #4
🌾
Support Diverse Local Producers at All Business Stages
  • Hands-on educational opportunities & mentoring networks
  • Develop shared processing, storage & distribution infrastructure
  • Increase land & water availability for young, beginning & diverse producers
