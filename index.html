Dashboard Purpose & Context

This dashboard visualizes key findings, community feedback, and action priorities from the Poudre Food Partnership's USDA Regional Food System Partnership (RFSP) Planning Grant. Over two years, our coalition of six organizations engaged local residents and producers to map challenges and opportunities. The data presented here serves as a collaborative roadmap to build a more equitable, vibrant, and resilient local food system in Northern Colorado.

Goal: Locally grown food accounts for 10% of all food purchased in the region by 2027
Right now, locally grown food makes up just 1% of what Northern Colorado residents buy. The goal is to grow that to 10% — roughly $200M+ in local food sales annually based on regional food expenditures. To be clear: this is about demand — getting consumers to choose and access locally produced food. Note: "local ag" includes hay, grains, and commodity feeds, but the primary opportunity for community-scale impact is in diversified fruits, vegetables, meat, and dairy sold directly to households, restaurants, and institutions.

🏪 The Single Greatest Opportunity: Supermarkets & Big-Box Stores

Since 85% of lower-income and 70% of higher-income residents rely on supermarkets and big-box stores, getting local food into these channels would reach everyone — dramatically lowering price, access, and familiarity barriers compared to farmers markets.

The real question isn't why don't residents visit farmers markets?
It's: why don't more local farmers sell to grocery stores?

Key supply-side barriers: minimum volume requirements (100+ units/week), lack of packaging & labeling infrastructure, and no shared cold-chain logistics. These infrastructure gaps — not consumer indifference — are what truly block local food from reaching every household.

🎯 Section 6

Next Steps: Strategic Action Priorities

Four strategic goals with specific, concrete actions and tactics — synthesized from community surveys, producer assessments, working groups, and focus groups.

How these actions support the 10% Market Share Goal ($15M local sales):
Our roadmap acts as a unified system to scale both demand and supply:
  • Food Sovereignty & Access builds consistent, high-volume consumer demand by addressing physical and financial access in low-income and Spanish-speaking communities (which make up a large portion of the region's population but shop mostly at big-box stores).
  • Community Outreach & Education drives purchasing habits by highlighting local brands, explaining agricultural labels, and engaging youth in schools and on farms.
  • Production Growth & Land Access scales up the local agricultural capacity (through land security, water storage, and shared storage/processing infrastructure) needed to fulfill this 10x increase in demand.

Goal #1
✊
Elevate & Empower the Voices of Marginalized People & Groups
  • Form an Independent Food Equity Council: Establish an autonomous community-led group to advocate directly to municipal governments for food-desert zoning changes and bilingual services.
  • Secure Municipal Zoning & Master Plan Integration: Partner with Loveland, Fort Collins, and Larimer County to write food access priorities into master plans, easing restrictions on mobile food markets and urban agriculture.
  • Secure Long-Term Member Funding (5+ Years): Establish recurring city/county grants to pay community members and bilingual promotoras for their advisory time, ensuring sustainable operations.
🤲 Food Sovereignty & Access
Goal #2
🥬
Increase Access to Local Foods Among All Community Members
  • Expand SNAP/WIC Outlets at Mobile Farm Stands: Provide hardware and staffing at mobile farm stands and neighborhood kiosks to accept food assistance and Double Up Food Bucks.
  • Bridge the Big-Box Grocery Gap: Build a shared aggregation facility so small local farms can combine products to meet the volume and delivery consistency required by major supermarkets.
  • Launch Decentralized Consignment Food Hubs: Deploy shipping-container hubs and mobile food trucks directly into USDA-designated food deserts to make local products physically accessible.
🤲 🗣️ Food Sovereignty + Outreach
Goal #3
📚
Increase Consumer Education & Awareness of the Local Food Economy
  • Integrate Food Systems in School Curricula: Partner with the Poudre School District to add local agriculture lessons to science classes, reinforced by funded farm tours and work days to close the loop for families.
  • Humanize Local Farms via Stories & Newsletters: Run public campaigns (like the Sugar Beet Substack) and meet-your-farmer events to build brand loyalty and increase direct-to-consumer sales.
  • Maintain Bilingual Local Food Directories: Create and update a Spanish/English mapping tool of active CSA farms, farm stands, and food pantries.
🗣️ Community Outreach & Education
Goal #4
🌾
Support Diverse Local Producers at All Business Stages
  • Launch Peer Mentoring & Business Training: Offer hands-on business and grant-writing classes to help beginning farmers transition from basic production to financial viability.
  • Build Shared Cold Storage & Processing Infrastructure: Establish a cooperative facility with washing, packing, and freezing equipment to help producers scale up to wholesale markets.
  • Secure Public Farmland & Water Leases: Partner with local municipal open space programs to lease buffer lands and agricultural water to beginning, young, and diverse producers.
